Galactic Aztec Heavy (Mk II)

SDSU Rocket Project

Galactic Aztec Mk II started in May of 2015 and was over a 2.5 year long project. It was 24ft long and had a dry weight of approximately 250 lbs in the end, giving it the nickname, Galactic Aztec Heavy (GAH). Over fifty students worked on Galactic Aztec Mark II and it served as a test bed for our developing Thrust Vectoring Control (TVC) and Reaction Control System (RCS) technologies. The last attempted launch date was on Sept 16, 2017, where the project came to an end due to an over-pressurization event and rupture of our liquid oxygen tank at T-minus 30 seconds.

I entered this project in February of 2016 and became Design Lead later on that May. I was a key member in the successful Static Hot Fire and TVC test on March 18th, 2017 (SHF-C). I oversaw and assisted in preparation and operation of the Igniter, Pressurization systems, Camera control, and Avionics. One of my most exciting/stressful moments in my life was being the one in the underground bunker at SHF-C operating the mission control software and hitting the buttons for Ignition T-Minus 0.
