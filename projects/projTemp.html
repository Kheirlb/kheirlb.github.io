STATIC HOT FIRES

The SDSU Rocket Project entered to compete in the FAR/MARS Launch Contest in September of 2017. We developed a cryogenic, bi-propelleant liquid test stand, capable of hot firing our first Liquid Oxygen/Liquid Methane Engine. During my tenure as President, we successfully Static Hot Fired our engine on 2 occasions, February 4th (SHF-A) and March 3rd (SHF-B), 2018.

I developed a comprehensive test preparation campaign involving multiple cryogenic (cold-flow) tests. I dramaticlly improved our procedures and managment processes in order to be able to succeed. The test stand was assembled in 3 months.

The SDSU Rocket Project went on to complete two more hot fires in our engine test campaign. You can learn more here.
