Static Hot Fires A & B

SDSU Rocket Project

The SDSU Rocket Project entered to compete in the FAR/Mars Launch Contest in September of 2017. After our PDR (Preliminary Design Review), we developed a cryogenic, bi-propelleant liquid test stand, capable of hot firing our first Liquid Oxygen/Liquid Methane Engine. During my tenure as President, we successfully Static Hot Fired our engine on 2 occasions in 2018, February 4th (SHF-A) and March 3rd (SHF-B).

During development, I created a comprehensive test preparation campaign involving multiple cryogenic (cold-flow) tests and procedural reviews. I dramaticlly improved our engineering and managment processes in order to be able to succeed. The test stand was assembled in 3 months and I can tell you the micro-details on almost every system.

One of the largest benefits of this project, is that it gave us the ability to teach and mentor our new members in basic structural and manufacturing development, fluid system design and assembly, and avionics system development and reliability.

The SDSU Rocket Project went on to complete two more hot fires in our engine test campaign before retiring the test stand. Our team successfully hot fired the launch vehicle in October of 2019 and have scheduled a new launch date for early 2020.
