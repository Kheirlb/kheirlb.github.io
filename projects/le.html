Lady Elizabeth

The Lady Elizabeth project began in September of 2017 as we entered the FAR/Mars Launch Contest. The goal of the contest is to be the first university team to launch a liquid-methane and liquid-oxygen rocket to 45,000’ altitude under FAR’s Class 2 FAA waiver impulse limit.

This project encompasses many years of work including our first test stand and student designed engine to our first LOX/LCH4 launch vehicle. I poured my heart and soul into this project over the past 2.5 years. The project began under my leadership as President and I continued to support the project as a Senior Engineer. Our major accomplishments include some of the following:

  • We introduced new design processes and succeeded in our first PDR and CDR.
  • Incorporated new project management tools and strategies (ASANA/Trello/Jira).
  • Our first LOX/LCH4 student designed engine.
  • 4 successful engine hot fires (the first 2 under my leadership).
  • 1 successful launch vehicle hot fire.
  • Largest budget ever managed for our club.
  • Thousands of electrical and fluid connections.

We have a scheduled launch in the early 2020 and I plan to be there to see it fly.
